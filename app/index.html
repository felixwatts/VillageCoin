VillageCoin is a platform upon which you can create your own Democratic Currency. That's a phrase I made up to denote a currency in which monetary and fiscal policy are set democratically by the users on a one user; one vote basis.

Unlike most currencies, VillageCoin currencies (aka villages) can implement arbitrary restrictions on account ownership (aka citizenship). This is primarily used to prevent sybil attacks by enforcing a one person; one account policy, but can also be used to restrict the currency to any subset of actors, for example:

  • Humans
  • Reddit users
  • Freemasons

Each village has a trusted set of citizens called gatekeepers who are charged with implementing the access policy of the village, as specified by the citizens. The gatekeepers assess applications to join the village from aspiring citizens and may grant or deny access.

The citizens of the village control everything else by a democratic system of referendums. They can do things like:

  • Create money
  • Set tax policy
  • Spend public funds
  • Appoint and dismiss gatekeepers

I'm not sure what any of this is for, it's just something I made in my spare time, but it could be a way to experiment with monetary and fiscal policy in a safe sandbox
